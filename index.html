📜 CHAMA CONSTITUTION

This Constitution outlines the rules, structure, and operations of the chama and shall guide all members.

👥 Article 1: Membership

  • Registration and Records:
    • Every member must register officially with the chama and provide accurate personal and contact details.
    • Members are required to update their information whenever changes occur.
  • Admission of Members:
    • New members may only join after approval by existing members or leadership.
    • This ensures trust, accountability, and harmony within the group.
  • Active Membership Requirement:
    • Membership shall be confirmed after a minimum of four (4) months of active participation.
    • Active participation includes consistent contributions, engagement in meetings, and compliance with chama rules.
  • 🔄 Merry-Go-Round Participation Rule:
    • Members are encouraged to participate in the merry-go-round system as part of group financial support.
    • 📌 Members who are not participating in the merry-go-round are required to actively contribute to savings without fail.
    • This ensures fairness and guarantees that all members remain eligible for welfare and other chama benefits.
    • Failure to participate in either savings or contributions may affect a member’s active status and benefits.
  • 🆕 Registration Fee (Effective January 2026):
    • All new members shall pay a one-time registration fee of Ksh 200.
    • This fee shall be used for administrative and logistical support, especially during physical meetings.
  • 💡 Contribution Categories (From 2026):
    • 💎 Category A: Weekly contribution of Ksh 200 + minimum savings of Ksh 50. Estimated monthly savings: Ksh 200.
    • 💼 Category B: Contribution of Ksh 500 twice a month + minimum savings of Ksh 50. Estimated monthly savings: Ksh 300.
  • 📌 General Principle:
    • All members must remain active through either savings or merry-go-round participation.
    • Consistency, discipline, and commitment define full membership eligibility and benefits.

💰 Article 2: Savings & Contributions

  • Contribution Requirement:
    • Every member must contribute according to their chosen category without fail.
    • All contributions must be made consistently to ensure smooth operation of the chama.
  • Importance of Savings:
    • Savings above the minimum are highly encouraged as they improve financial stability and increase loan eligibility.
    • Consistent saving reflects discipline, commitment, and trustworthiness within the chama.
  • ⏰ Payment Deadlines:
    • All weekly or bi-monthly contributions must be submitted on or before the agreed deadlines.
    • Timely payments ensure smooth financial planning and group stability.
  • ⚠️ Penalties for Late Payment:
    • Failure to pay by the deadline shall attract a fine of Ksh 30.
    • The fine will be added to the member’s account and must be cleared accordingly.
  • 📊 Transparency and Records:
    • All savings and contribution records shall be transparent and accessible to members at any time.
    • Members are encouraged to regularly check their records for accuracy.
  • 💞 Welfare Eligibility Requirement:
    • To qualify for any welfare support, a member must have total savings of at least Ksh 1,500 and above.
    • Members with savings below this threshold shall not be eligible for welfare benefits such as emergency support, wedding, or funeral assistance.
    • This rule ensures fairness and sustainability of the chama welfare fund.
  • 🔄 Merry-Go-Round Participation Safety Rule:
    • Members are encouraged to actively participate in the merry-go-round system to enjoy full group benefits.
    • 📌 Members whose savings are below the required minimum or below agreed contribution levels may not receive merry-go-round payouts early.
    • This measure is put in place to ensure financial safety, fairness, and sustainability of the chama funds.
    • Priority in merry-go-round payouts shall be given to members who are consistent in savings and contributions.
    • 📌 Saving Discipline:
      • Members are strongly encouraged to save consistently in order to strengthen their financial position within the chama.

    • 🏆 Savings Reward System:
        ```
      • Members with total savings of 1500 or more and active for at least 10 months shall qualify for the highest reward rate of 79.9%, while the remaining 20.1% shall be retained as group capital.
      • Members with total savings of 1500 or more and active for at least 6 months shall qualify for a 74.99% reward rate, while the remaining 25.01% shall be retained as group capital.
      • Members with total savings of 1000 or more but who do not meet the above conditions shall qualify for a 30% reward rate, while the remaining 70% shall be retained as group capital.
      • 📌 Reward percentages may be reviewed depending on the financial position and sustainability of the group.
      ```

    • 📢 Exit Settlement:
        ```
      • Any member who exits the chama shall receive 100% of their savings after clearing all outstanding obligations.
      ```

    🏦 Article 3: Loans

    • Loan Eligibility:
      • A member qualifies for a loan after saving at least Ksh 1,500.
      • The member must have been active in the chama for at least four (4) months.
      • Active participation includes consistent savings, contributions, and adherence to chama rules.
    • Loan Assessment Criteria:
      • Loans shall be issued based on the member’s savings history and consistency.
      • Discipline and commitment within the chama shall be considered.
      • The member’s ability to repay within the agreed period will also be assessed.
    • 📌 Loan Approval:
      • All loan requests must be approved by both the Admin and Chairlady.
      • Approval shall ensure fairness, accountability, and proper fund management.
      • The leadership reserves the right to reject or adjust loan requests based on available funds.
    • ⏳ Loan Repayment Terms:
      • All loans must be repaid within the agreed timeframe.
      • Late repayment may attract penalties or restrictions on future borrowing.
      • Repeated default may lead to suspension of loan privileges.
    • 💸 Interest Distribution Policy:
      • Upon loan repayment, the interest earned shall be shared as follows:
      • 🌿 25% is credited back to the borrower’s personal savings account.
      • 🤝 75% is distributed among all members based on their savings ratio.
    • 📘 Fairness and Sustainability:
      • This system ensures fairness among members and rewards consistent savers.
      • It also strengthens the financial stability and growth of the chama.
      • All loan activities shall be recorded transparently for accountability.

    🧑‍💼 Article 4: Leadership

    • The chama shall have two key leadership roles:
    • 💰 Founder & Admin Roles:
      • Oversee all financial management of the chama.
      • Maintain accurate records of savings, contributions, and balances.
      • Control and safeguard all group funds.
      • Approve and process loan requests together with the Chairlady.
      • Track loan repayments and ensure compliance with terms.
      • Communicate important updates, deadlines, and announcements.
      • Prepare and present financial reports to members.
      • Ensure transparency and accountability in all financial matters.
      • Coordinate overall operations of the chama.
    • 👩‍💼 Chairlady Roles:
      • Represent the interests and welfare of all members.
      • Coordinate group activities, meetings, and discussions.
      • Work closely with the Admin to ensure smooth operations.
      • Assist in approval of loans and welfare requests.
      • Ensure discipline, unity, and fairness among members.
      • Handle member concerns, conflicts, and feedback.
      • Mobilize members for meetings and participation.
      • Support enforcement of the constitution and rules.
    • General Leadership Provisions:
      • The Chairlady shall be elected by members.
      • The term of service shall be one (1) year.
      • Members may re-elect or extend the term based on performance.
      • The Admin and Chairlady shall work together to ensure transparency, fairness, and unity.
      • Leadership must ensure proper tracking of contributions, savings, loans, and welfare matters.
      • Regular updates on finances and chama activities shall be shared with all members.

    💞 Article 5: Welfare

    • 🕊️ Bereavement Support (Funeral Assistance):
      • In the event of death (parent, guardian, spouse, or immediate family), the chama shall provide Ksh 3,000 from group savings.
      • Members must notify the Chairlady or Admin at least one (1) week before the burial to allow proper arrangements and group participation.
    • 💍 Wedding Support:
      • In the event of a wedding, the chama shall provide Ksh 2,500 from group savings.
      • The member must notify the Chairlady or Admin at least three (3) months before the wedding date for proper planning and support coordination.
    • 👶 Newborn Baby Support:
      • In the event of a newborn baby, the chama shall provide Ksh 2,000 from group savings.
      • The member must notify the Chairlady or Admin at least three (3) months in advance (or as early as pregnancy confirmation allows) for proper planning and support.
    • 🚑 Emergency Assistance:
      • In case of an emergency, a member with savings of at least Ksh 1,500 may receive up to 30% of their savings as assistance.
      • Recovery shall be done by deducting 20% from the member’s payout when their turn arrives.
      • This assistance is granted only once per cycle and must be approved by the Admin and Chairlady.
    • 📌 Merry-Go-Round Participation:
      • Members who are not participating in the merry-go-round are required to actively contribute to savings.
      • This ensures they remain eligible for welfare benefits and other chama support systems.
    • 🤝 Additional Support:
      • Additional assistance may be provided depending on agreement among members and availability of funds.
      • Each case shall be handled fairly and transparently by the leadership.
    • 📢 Notification Requirement:
      • Members must notify leadership early in advance for all planned welfare-related events.
      • This ensures proper budgeting, coordination, and participation from the chama.

    🤝 Article 6: Conduct

    • General Code of Conduct:
      • All members shall uphold respect, honesty, integrity, and transparency in all chama activities.
      • Members shall act in good faith and prioritize the unity and growth of the chama.
    • 🤝 Respect and Interpersonal Relations:
      • Members shall treat one another with respect, dignity, and fairness at all times.
      • Abusive language, harassment, or discrimination of any kind shall not be tolerated.
      • Members shall resolve disagreements peacefully and professionally.
    • 📅 Participation and Meetings:
      • Members are expected to attend all scheduled meetings and group activities.
      • In case of absence, members must communicate in advance to the leadership.
      • Active participation in discussions and decisions is encouraged.
    • 📢 Communication:
      • Members shall communicate clearly, honestly, and respectfully in all group platforms.
      • Official communication channels of the chama must be used for updates and announcements.
      • Misleading or false information shall not be shared.
    • 🔒 Confidentiality:
      • All group matters, including financial records and member information, shall remain confidential.
      • Members shall not disclose internal chama information to outsiders without approval.
    • 📌 Member Responsibility:
      • Members shall take responsibility for their financial commitments and obligations.
      • Members shall comply with all rules outlined in this constitution.
      • Any issues or concerns shall be reported promptly to the leadership.

    ⚖️ Article 7: Discipline

    • General Principle:
      • All members are expected to uphold discipline, responsibility, and commitment to the chama.
      • Failure to follow the constitution and agreed rules shall attract appropriate action.
    • 📌 Failure to Meet Obligations:
      • Late or missed contributions shall attract penalties as defined in Article 2.
      • Repeated failure to contribute may lead to warnings and possible suspension.
      • Members with continuous default may lose eligibility for loans and welfare benefits.
    • ⏳ Inactive Members:
      • Members who remain inactive without communication may be issued a warning.
      • Continued inactivity may lead to temporary suspension.
      • Prolonged inactivity may result in removal from the chama.
    • ⚠️ Misconduct and Behavior:
      • Any form of disrespect, dishonesty, or conflict among members is prohibited.
      • Fraud, misuse of funds, or intentional misinformation shall lead to strict disciplinary action.
      • Actions that damage the reputation or unity of the chama shall not be tolerated.
    • 🚫 Disciplinary Measures:
      • Verbal or written warning.
      • Fines or penalties.
      • Suspension from group activities or benefits.
      • Permanent removal from the chama.
    • ⚖️ Right to Fair Hearing:
      • Every member shall be given an opportunity to explain themselves before any major action is taken.
      • Decisions shall be made fairly by the Admin and Chairlady, with member consultation where necessary.

    🔁 Article 8: Exit Policy

    • Definition of Exit:
      • A member shall be considered to have exited the chama upon full withdrawal of their savings.
      • Exit may be voluntary or as a result of disciplinary action.
    • 📢 Notice Requirement:
      • Members intending to exit must notify the Admin in advance.
      • Notice should be given at least 7 days before the intended withdrawal date.
      • This allows proper financial planning and record updates.
    • 📋 Clearance of Obligations:
      • All outstanding loans must be fully repaid before exit.
      • Any pending fines or penalties must be cleared.
      • The member must settle all obligations with the chama before payment is processed.
    • 💰 Processing of Payments:
      • All payments shall be processed within 7 days after official notification and clearance.
      • The exiting member shall receive 100% of their savings after deductions (if any).
      • Payments shall be recorded and confirmed by both the Admin and Chairlady.
    • ⚠️ Limitations:
      • An exited member shall lose access to all chama benefits, including loans and welfare support.
      • Rejoining the chama shall be treated as a new membership and subject to approval.

    ✔️ This Constitution is binding to all members upon approval.
